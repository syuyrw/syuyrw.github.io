Design Principles

White Space

Mcdonalds

The principle of white space is the idea that good design doesn't fill every possible space with content. The Mcdonalds home page is a great example of white space. Looking at the above screenshot you can see that the design looks clean and easy on the eyes because they don't overwhelm the user with tons of content, but rather they display what is necessary, with plenty of space in between each different piece of content.

Rule Of Thirds

GV

The principle of the rule of thirds is the idea that things are visually more pleasing to look at when they are layed out in thirds. If you look at the screenshot above, you can see that the website for GV Capital is a great example of the rule of thirds. All of the different bits of content are placed along the different thirds of the screen in a very clean and concise way.

Visual Heirarchy

YouTube

The principle of the rule of Visual Heirarchy is a very important one for businesses. It's the idea that the eye goes to the biggest, or most eye catching item on the screen first. YouTube uses this concept by making it so that the video thumbnails are the biggest thing on the screen, because that is the product they offer. If you click on a video thumbnail, they make money off of ad revenue.
